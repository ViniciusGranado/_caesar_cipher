About Caesar Cipher

In cryptography, a Caesar cipher, also known as Caesar's cipher, or the shift cipher, is one of the simplest and most widely known encryption techniques. It is a type of substitution cipher in which each letter in the plaintext is replaced by a letter some fixed number of positions down the alphabet. This position is called "Key". For example, with a key of 3, A would be replaced by D, B would become E, and so on. The method is named after Julius Caesar, who used it in his private correspondence.
